Mission

Our mission is to build a sustainable future in aviation by developing high-performance, eco-friendly light aircraft solutions that address the global emissions challenge.

Who are we ?

Pullinam Aerospace Technologies is an Indian startup aiming to lead light aircraft manufacturing and reduce aviation’s carbon footprint by developing autonomous, eco-friendly aircraft.

Vision

We are committed to reducing global emissions through innovative aircraft designs and propulsion systems, including electric, sustainable aviation fuel (SAF), and other non-electric solutions.

Pullinam

The name “Pullinam” comes from an ancient Tamil word meaning a species of bird, found in Tamil literary classics such as Silappathigaram, Seevaga Sinthamani, and Kalithogai.

Inspiration

This reflects our commitment to lightweight, agile, and environmentally conscious designs inspired by nature, embodied in our use of the Emerald Dove as a model.

Uniqueness

We are dedicated to reducing carbon emissions through the development of electric propulsion and the use of Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) in our aircraft.

C.S. Karunakaran

Founder & CEO

C. S. Karunakaran, widely known as Professor Karunakaran in the Indian aerospace sector, comes from a middle-class Tamil family in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu. He is a distinguished academician, inventor, aircraft mechanic, drone expert, researcher, techno-commercial activist, professional body leader, startup coach, and public speaker. A strong advocate for nurturing and guiding young talent within a robust startup ecosystem, he believes this is essential for sustainable growth, nation-building, and global technological advancement. Throughout his career, Professor Karunakaran has mentored numerous successful deep-tech startups in fields such as drones, satellites, space launch vehicles, edutech, electric vehicles, aviation services, aerospace, and defense. He has been involved in over 100 product development and maintenance projects, contributing to innovations such as flying bikes, COVID-19 drone monitoring systems, defense drones, aircraft structural maintenance, aero engine restoration, and fixed-wing UAV designs. His leadership has been pivotal in driving key developments in various aerospace forums and professional societies. One of his significant achievements is the launch of collegiate drone contests through SAEINDIA, which has fostered innovation among young engineers. During his honorary tenure at the Aeronautical Society of India (AeSI) Chennai Branch and his leadership role at the Institution of Engineers India Tamil Nadu State Centre (IEI TNSC), he spearheaded numerous professional and industry development initiatives. His contributions to the Institute of Aeronautics Astronautics and Aviation (IAAA) have helped transform it into an active Indian engineering association. With over 150 lectures delivered and more than 40 national and international awards to his credit, Professor Karunakaran’s passion for aerospace is reflected in his tireless work ethic and innovative mindset. In July 2023, driven by his love for aircraft and commitment to fostering young talent, Professor Karunakaran founded Pullinam Aerospace Technologies Private Limited with the support of co-founder and AI & Data Analytics expert S. B. Shiva Prasath. Under his visionary leadership, the company embarked on its journey with the goal of manufacturing two-seater pilotless microlight aircraft powered by electric/IC engine propulsion. Pullinam Aerospace aims to become a key player in the global aviation market by 2050, focusing on light aircraft, SAF-based solutions, electric aircraft variants, VTOL (Vertical Take-Off and Landing), CTOL (Conventional Take-Off and Landing), and space tourism. Professor Karunakaran envisions Pullinam Aerospace as a future leader in delivering aviation solutions to regions including Africa, the Middle East, South Asia, and Southeast Asia.

Shri C U Hari

Director

Shri CU Hari, former Director of Aeronautics at DRDO Headquarters, New Delhi, and Secretary of the Aeronautical Research & Development Board (AR&DB) under the Ministry of Defence, has dedicated over 36 years to advancing India’s aerospace sector. Known for his vast expertise in aircraft structural design, production management, and corporate strategy, he played a key role in developing the LCA Flight Control System as Joint Project Director at the Aeronautical Development Establishment (ADE), Bangalore. At DRDO Headquarters, Shri Hari was instrumental in coordinating the activities of major aeronautics laboratories, such as ADE, ADRDE, CABS, DARE, GTRE, CEMILAC, and ADA, while serving as the primary link with Defence Services. Following his retirement, he continued his contributions to aerospace as Director of Administration and Contracts at the Aeronautical Development Agency (ADA), overseeing human resources, recruitment, and international contract negotiations, with project management experience across the USA, Russia, Germany, and Spain. His commitment to UAV innovation extended through his work at the Centre for Aerospace Research at Anna University, where he led teams in UAV design, testing, and quality assurance. Shri Hari’s leadership roles include serving as Secretary of the Aeronautical Society of India (Bangalore Branch), President of the Institute of Aeronautics, Astronautics, and Aviation, and Chairman of the Institute of Defence Scientists & Technologists. His areas of focus include UAV development, project management, budgeting, contract negotiations, and airworthiness certification, marking him as a prominent figure in India’s aerospace advancement. He has also held esteemed positions, including Chairman of the Quality Council of India (QCI) for Remotely Piloted Aircraft Systems Scheme and Chief Consultant at the Centre for Aerospace Research at MIT Campus, Anna University.

Dr. G Ramesh

Director

Dr. G Ramesh brings over 36 years of experience, including his role as Scientist in Experimental Aerodynamics and Senior Scientist, Head of the MAV division at the National Aerospace Laboratory, Bengaluru. He has also served as a Visiting Professor at Jain University and as Dean of Research and Head of Aeronautical Engineering at GCEM. Recently, he was a Technical Advisor for the DRONE Division at Gopalan Aerospace. With over 90 publications and 50 invited talks on Advanced Flow Diagnostics and Micro Air Vehicles, he is a recognized thought leader in the field of Aeronautics and Aircraft Design. He was also a member of the Aeronautics Research Development Board UAV panel, SIGMA panel, and served as a technical advisor for UAV applications in the National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) and the Government of Karnataka's study on UAV applications for societal use. Additionally, he was a research council member at CSMCRI, Bhavnagar.
